Information Collection and Use

FlashMind does NOT collect any personally identifiable information. All your flashcards, decks, study progress, and personal content remain on your device and are never transmitted to any external servers owned by us.

The app stores the following information locally on your device only:

  • Flashcards (front and back text) you create or import
  • Deck names, descriptions, colors, and organization
  • Study progress, spaced repetition intervals, and mastery levels
  • Study session history and statistics (streaks, accuracy, time spent)
  • Your app preferences (language, theme mode, daily goal, notification settings)
  • Notification schedules for study reminders

Your flashcards, study data, and personal content never leave your device and are not transmitted to any servers owned by KasumiLabs.

Community Decks (Cloud Feature)

FlashMind offers an optional Community Decks feature that allows users to share and discover flashcard decks published by other users. This feature uses Google Cloud Firestore to store and retrieve shared decks.

What data is shared when you publish a deck?

  • The deck name, description, and category you provide
  • The flashcard content (front and back text) within the shared deck
  • An anonymous device identifier (not linked to your personal identity)
  • Timestamp of when the deck was published

Publishing is entirely optional. You are never required to share any decks. Your private decks, study progress, statistics, and personal preferences are never uploaded to the cloud. Only decks you explicitly choose to publish are shared.

What data is collected when you browse community decks?

  • Standard Firestore read operations (no personal data is collected)
  • Downloaded community decks are stored locally on your device

Advertising

The Application displays advertisements served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect and use data to show you personalized or non-personalized ads based on your consent preferences.

What data may AdMob collect?

  • Device identifiers (advertising ID)
  • IP address
  • General location data (country/region level)
  • App usage and interaction data with ads

GDPR Consent: For users in the European Economic Area (EEA), the Application uses Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP) to request your consent before displaying personalized advertisements. You can change your consent preferences at any time through the Application settings or by reinstalling the app.

Important: Your flashcards, study data, decks, and personal content are NEVER shared with ad networks. Only anonymous device and usage data is shared for the purpose of serving ads.

Camera and OCR

The Application offers an optional feature to scan text from books, notes, or printed materials using your device's camera to quickly create flashcards. This feature uses:

  • Camera Access: To capture images of text — books, notes, whiteboards, or any printed material
  • Google ML Kit (On-Device): To perform text recognition (OCR) entirely on your device

All image processing is performed locally on your device. Camera images and scanned text are NOT uploaded to any server. Google ML Kit runs entirely on-device and does not transmit image data to Google servers.

Firebase Services

What data does Firebase collect?

  • Device information (model, OS version, screen size)
  • App usage data (screens viewed, features used, session duration)
  • Crash reports and error logs (to help us fix bugs)
  • Anonymous analytics data (no personally identifiable information)

This data is collected automatically by Firebase and helps us:

  • Identify and fix bugs and crashes
  • Improve app stability and performance
  • Understand which features are most useful to users
  • Understand which devices and OS versions need optimization

Important: Crash logs do NOT contain your flashcards, study progress, deck content, or personal content. They only contain technical information about what went wrong in the app.

Permissions

The Application may request the following permissions:

  • Camera: To scan text and create flashcards using OCR (optional — you can always create cards manually or import from CSV)
  • Storage: To import flashcards from CSV files stored on your device
  • Notifications: To send daily study reminders and streak warning notifications
  • Exact Alarms: To schedule precise notification delivery at the times you configure
  • Internet: Required for the optional community decks feature, ads, and crash reporting

These permissions are used solely for the app's core functionality. We do not access, collect, or transmit your personal files or data through these permissions. All permissions are optional and the app remains functional without them.

Data Storage

All your personal data (flashcards, decks, study progress, preferences) is stored locally on your device. We do not use cloud storage for your private data. This means:

  • Your data is as secure as your device
  • Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all locally stored data
  • We cannot access, recover, or view your private data
  • Community decks you published to Firestore can be deleted from within the app

Log Data

In case of an error in the Application, we collect data and information through Firebase Crashlytics called Log Data. This Log Data may include information such as your device Internet Protocol ("IP") address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of the app when utilizing our Application, the time and date of your use, crash logs, and other diagnostic information.

Important: Log Data does NOT contain your flashcards, study progress, deck content, or any personal content you have entered into the Application.

Your Data Rights (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), if you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights. Since all your personal data is stored locally on your device, you have full control:

  • Access: View all your flashcards, decks, and study statistics within the app
  • Rectification: Edit any flashcard or deck information at any time
  • Erasure: Delete individual cards, entire decks, or all data through the app
  • Complete Removal: Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored data
  • Community Decks: You can delete any decks you have published to the community from within the app
  • Consent Management: Manage your advertising consent preferences through the consent dialog shown at app startup
  • Opt-out Analytics: You can limit data collection by disabling analytics in your device settings
